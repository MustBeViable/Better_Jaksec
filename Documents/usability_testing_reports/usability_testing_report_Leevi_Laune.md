# What I Learned from Usability Testing

Based on the usability tests of Finna, MyAT, and HSL, I gained several important insights into user behavior, interface design, and common usability issues.

---

## 1. Users rely heavily on immediate clarity and visual cues

Across all three services, it was clear that users quickly form impressions based on visual design and layout.

- In **MyAT**, the image and overall layout influenced assumptions about the target audience.
- In **Finna**, good contrast improved readability, but missing images caused confusion and frustration.
- In **HSL**, clear navigation helped users move through tasks efficiently.

**Key learning:**  
Even small visual inconsistencies or missing elements can strongly affect how users understand and trust a service.

---

## 2. Search functionality is critical

Search was one of the most frequently used and important features.

- In **Finna**, users immediately used the search bar and successfully found books by title and author.
- In **HSL**, users relied on search to find routes and transportation options.

**Key learning:**  
Search must be highly visible, intuitive, and flexible, since users often rely on it as their primary way to interact with a system.

---

## 3. Missing or unclear information leads to frustration

Users often struggled when expected information was not easily available.

- In **Finna**, users could not easily find availability or information on where to read the book.
- In **HSL**, ticket pricing was not visible in the route flow and had to be found separately.
- In **MyAT**, the “About us” page was unclear and did not meet user expectations.

**Key learning:**  
Important information should be available at the exact moment the user needs it, not hidden in separate sections.

---

## 4. Navigation must be consistent and meaningful

Navigation issues appeared in multiple services:

- In **MyAT**, inconsistent naming (e.g., “My AT”) caused confusion.
- In **Finna**, users struggled to find additional information links.
- In **HSL**, users eventually found ticket prices, but not through an intuitive flow.

**Key learning:**  
Navigation labels must be clear, consistent, and aligned with user expectations to avoid confusion.

---

## 5. Technical issues and feedback affect user trust

- In **Finna**, missing images created a negative impression and reduced trust.
- In **HSL**, location permissions failed in one browser, forcing the user to switch browsers.

**Key learning:**  
Even small technical problems can disrupt the user journey and reduce confidence in the system.

---

## 6. Users expect context-aware information

Users expected relevant information to appear within their workflow:

- Book availability in **Finna**
- Ticket prices during route planning in **HSL**
- Clear purpose and explanation in **MyAT**

**Key learning:**  
Information should be integrated into the user flow instead of being separated into unrelated pages or sections.

---

## Overall Reflection

From these usability tests, I learned that usability is not only about whether a system works, but about how clearly and efficiently it communicates with the user.

Users expect:
- Immediate understanding of the interface
- Easy and reliable search
- Clear and consistent navigation
- Contextual information when making decisions
- Stable and error-free technical performance

Even small usability issues can significantly impact the overall user experience.
